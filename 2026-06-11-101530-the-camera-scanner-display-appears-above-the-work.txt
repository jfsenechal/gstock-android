  What was wrong

  The insets were fine (the Scaffold already pads the NavHost), and
  QrScannerView already uses TextureView mode. The real problem was the layout 
  of ScanToolsStep: it packed the worker card, two text blocks, a greedy
  weight(1f) camera, a scrollable list, and two buttons into one non-scrollable
  Column.

  The preview device is tall enough that everything fits. On a real phone вҖ”
  which is shorter and loses height to the top app bar and bottom nav bar вҖ” the
  fixed sections no longer fit. The camera's weighted slot collapses toward
  zero, so the blocks pile up and overlap, exactly as in your screenshot.

  The fix (CheckoutScreen.kt, ScanToolsStep)

  Guided by the edge-to-edge / adaptive skills:

  1. Responsive camera height вҖ” wrapped the step in BoxWithConstraints and sized
  the camera to 40% of the actual viewport, clamped to 200dpвҖҰ360dp, instead of
  a greedy weight(1f) that fights the other content for space. On a tall screen
  it looks like your preview; on a short one it stays a sensible size.
  2. Scrollable step вҖ” the content Column now has verticalScroll(...), so if
  everything can't fit, it scrolls instead of overlapping. This is what
  guarantees no overlap on any screen size.
  3. Plain Column for the cart instead of LazyColumn вҖ” the cart is small, and a
  nested lazy list would fight the outer scroll for the same vertical gesture
  (and heightIn(max=180) is no longer needed since the page scrolls).

  The previews are unchanged in structure and still render correctly. Worth
  re-running on the device to confirm the overlap is gone.
